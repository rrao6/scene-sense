# Legal considerations — open research

**Owner:** Rahul Rao to coordinate with Nicole Lodge.

> MVP is CTV-only, HITL-approved, pre-generated content. Send-to-phone / dual-screen legal review sequencing is P2.

## Confirmed stance (2026-05-05 planning)
- Legal wants to approve **each use case individually** before enabling monetization.
- Avoid competitive-brand adjacency (e.g., Coca-Cola ad during a Pepsi scene).

## Open asks for legal

### MVP (CTV pause) primitives — priority for review
1. SceneIQ card (grounded fact)
2. BTS / trivia (fact)
3. Trivia (MCQ)
4. Cast / "Recognize her?"
5. Explainer ("Explain this scene") — editorial-only at launch
6. "How real is it?" (legal / medical / historical / book-accuracy)
7. Cameo alert
8. Easter eggs / foreshadowing
9. Title sponsorship chrome ("Brought to you by…")
10. Pre-roll buyout / title takeover

### P1.5 — after MVP legal sign-off
- Poll (opinion) — aggregate reveal
- Character / relationship maps
- "Which Character Are You?" polls

### P2 — not in MVP legal scope
- Send-to-phone flows per partner: Fandango, Amazon storefront, DoorDash / Instacart / GoPuff, Best Network wallet
- Shoulder content (inherits from source — confirm licensing)
- Mobile / web SceneSense surfaces

## Cross-cutting asks
- Source citation requirements — when do we need to show a citation?
- Cast-related content — right-of-publicity constraints on generated claims (net worth, personal life, relationships).
- Defamation / false-claim exposure on "How real is it?" content — expert-framed vs. claim-making language.
- Spoiler / foreshadowing UX — any content-licensing constraints that gate these?
- Competitive-brand detection process: pipeline auto-check of scene tags + brand mentions against advertiser category, or ops-manual step at launch?
- Children / audience protections: moderation-tag gating, COPPA implications for shows with under-13 audiences.
- International scope — which markets in-scope at MVP; which we explicitly exclude.

## Dependency on ad servers
- Adrise click tracking and poll analytics are out of scope for Interactive Pause MVP; same for SceneSense sponsored primitives until engagement data justifies the work.
