SceneIQ
Architecture overview

SceneIQ turns scene understanding into verified prompts and learning signals

A source-grounded generation stack creates prompt candidates, validates evidence, re-checks citations, then ships a client-ready bundle whose engagement data feeds ML and ads systems.

1. Input

Tubi Moments VLM

Scenes, cast, dialogue, objects, themes, songs, and wiki-matched trivia.

Domain + eligibility

Classify legal, historical, medical, military, hacking, rom-com, etc.; skip flagged or short scenes.

3. Validate + score

Generation-time gates

Google grounding URLs only, quote match, source blacklist, expert gate, MCQ option-kind checks.

Finalizer

Semantic dedup, 7-dim scorecard, verdict logic: approve, reject, or needs-edit.

4. Eval

Live re-fetch

Re-fetch cited URLs and verify title grounding still holds.

Quote re-match

Re-match evidence verbatim and feed the accuracy score before output.

5. Output

Client bundle

ui.json contract: actorFact, sceneFact, actorTrivia, sceneTrivia.

Audit + HITL

final.json, review.json, and prompts/HITL review with verdicts and evidence.

Post-click feedback loop: prompt opens, actor clicks, watchlist adds, and title jumps become ranking features, user-profile depth, recommendation lift, and partner/ads intelligence.

User engagement

What did the user click, expand, ask about, skip, save, or follow into another title?

ML signals

Cast affinity, topic depth, brand/object interest, genre nuance, and entity graph updates.

Business outcomes

Better recommendations, richer identity profiles, higher-intent segments, and partner insights.
