SceneIQ
Problem

Viewer curiosity already exists. Tubi just does not capture it yet.

People already search actors, songs, outfits, trivia, and “is this real?” moments while watching. SceneIQ brings that behavior back into the Tubi experience instead of letting it happen off-platform.

Viewer behavior

Curiosity leaves the player

Viewers pause, pull out their phone, search Google, open IMDb, or do nothing. The intent exists, but Tubi does not own the moment.

Business surface

A new premium surface is missing

In-player and pause-state curiosity can become useful, opt-in surface area for contextual partners without feeling like another ad break.

Data advantage

Intent data is lost

Searches, saves, actor interest, product curiosity, and follow-up questions could improve recommendations, ML ranking, user profiles, and ads targeting.

Mobile handoff: the second-screen habit is already happening. SceneIQ gives viewers a reason to download or re-open Tubi when they want to continue the interaction.

Product experience

Prompts become useful moments inside playback

The product surface is not a generic overlay. It is contextual, scene-aware, and designed for optional interaction.

In-player prompt

Contextual brand moment

Discovery follow-through

Engagement can inform the next title, rail, or discovery surface after the prompt.
